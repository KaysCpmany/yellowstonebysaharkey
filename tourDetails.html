Tour Overview

Discover the magic of Yellowstone National Park on this 5-day immersive adventure. Experience the world's largest collection of geysers, including the iconic Old Faithful. Witness breathtaking canyons, pristine lakes, and abundant wildlife including bison, elk, and bears in their natural habitat.

Our expert naturalist guides will lead you through the park's most spectacular landscapes while sharing fascinating insights about geology, ecology, and history. This tour combines comfortable accommodations with authentic wilderness experiences.

Daily Itinerary

1

Arrival & Grand Canyon of Yellowstone

Arrive at Yellowstone and meet your guide. After lunch, we'll head to the Grand Canyon of Yellowstone to witness the spectacular Lower Falls. Enjoy a short hike along the canyon rim for breathtaking views.

Hiking: 2 miles Dinner included Lodge at Canyon Village
2

Geyser Basins & Old Faithful

Explore the Upper Geyser Basin, home to the world-famous Old Faithful. Witness spectacular eruptions and colorful hot springs. After lunch, we'll hike to Solitary Geyser and enjoy the scenic Firehole River.

Hiking: 4 miles Photography Focus
3

Wildlife Safari & Yellowstone Lake

Early morning wildlife viewing in Hayden Valley. Spot bison, elk, and maybe wolves! Afternoon kayak adventure on Yellowstone Lake with stunning mountain views. Evening at leisure in Lake Village.

Wildlife Viewing Kayaking
4

Mammoth Hot Springs & Lamar Valley

Explore the unique terraces of Mammoth Hot Springs. Afternoon wildlife safari in Lamar Valley, known as America's Serengeti. Look for bears, wolves, and herds of bison in this spectacular valley.

Scenic Drive Wildlife Photography
5

Norris Geyser Basin & Departure

Visit the hottest and most dynamic thermal area in Yellowstone - Norris Geyser Basin. Enjoy a farewell lunch before departing with incredible memories of Yellowstone's wonders.

Hiking: 3 miles Lunch included

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know about the Yellowstone Explorer tour

What is the fitness level required for this tour?

This tour requires a moderate level of fitness. We'll be hiking 2-5 miles per day on uneven terrain at elevations of 7,000-8,000 feet. Participants should be comfortable walking for 2-3 hours at a time. The kayaking activity is suitable for beginners.

What should I pack for this tour?

We recommend layered clothing for variable weather conditions, sturdy hiking shoes, rain gear, sun protection (hat, sunglasses, sunscreen), a reusable water bottle, binoculars for wildlife viewing, and a camera. A detailed packing list will be provided upon booking.

What is the cancellation policy?

You can cancel your booking up to 30 days before departure for a full refund. Cancellations 15-29 days before departure receive a 50% refund. Cancellations within 14 days of departure are non-refundable. We strongly recommend purchasing travel insurance.

What wildlife might we see?

Yellowstone is home to diverse wildlife including bison, elk, moose, bears (black and grizzly), wolves, coyotes, foxes, eagles, and more. While sightings can't be guaranteed, our expert guides know the best locations and times for wildlife viewing.

How far in advance should I book?

We recommend booking at least 3-6 months in advance, especially for summer departures. Popular dates often fill up quickly. Last-minute bookings may be available, but we can't guarantee space.
